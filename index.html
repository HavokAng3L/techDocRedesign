Linked List

What is a linked list?

A linked list is a series of nodes connected together by their addresses in memory.

A node is a group of related information and consists of two parts

  • The data (int, char, string, etc)
  • The address to another node

Why use a linked list?

In general, data structures are used for processing, retrieving and storing data in a much quicker way.

Linked lists, specifically, are great for inserting and deleting data. The drawback however is to look-up a specific node, you must go through each node. From the start of the list.
